Carbonara
Carbonara is a pasta dish made with fatty cured pork, hard cheese, eggs, salt, and black pepper. It is typical of the Lazio region of Italy. The di sh took its modern form and name in the middle of the 20th century. The cheese is usually pecorino romano. Some varia tions use Parmesan, Grana Padano, or a combination of cheeses. Spaghetti is the most comm on pasta, but bucatini or rigatoni are also used.
